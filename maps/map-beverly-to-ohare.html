7 to 12 minutes faster, and the downtown double-back disappears. This corridor shows the draft schedule at its best: the X6→X5 connection at Union is actually timed (8:39 → 8:44), and the tunnel's combined frequency lets riders improvise — the X2 and X3 give the UP-NW corridor a train every 15 minutes, and either one plus the Blue Line at Jefferson Park beats everything. Beverly, which today has no direct rail to anywhere but LaSalle Street, gets the whole network.

Method & sources. Both networks routed with Conveyal R5 (via r5r 2.4.0), Tuesday July 7, 2026, ~8:00 am departures, same OpenStreetMap street network; today's feeds are published CTA/Metra/Pace GTFS, the scenario adds the CrossTowner X1–X6 scheduled feed generated from Star:Line Chicago's timetable. All three trips are measured to the Terminal 2 security line, charged 5 minutes' walk alike from the Blue Line platform and from the ATS T2 station; the ATS leg (~13 minutes from O'Hare Transfer) is composed from the City's published characteristics (every 3–5 minutes at peak, 24/7; 10 minutes end-to-end MMF→T1 — flychicago.com) since the ATS is in no GTFS feed, and its map geometry is the actual guideway from OpenStreetMap. The X6→X5 card is the scenario timetable verbatim; the Jefferson Park variant is R5's fastest routed itinerary. Times rounded to the minute. Station accessibility comes from the feeds' wheelchair_boarding field (all trains are accessible; per the analysis assumption, today's station accessibility carries into the future and new CrossTowner stations are accessible).
