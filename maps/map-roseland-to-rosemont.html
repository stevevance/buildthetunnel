Roseland to Rosemont: new rail nearby, but not on this trip

The Red Line Extension, under construction from 95th to 130th, will bring rapid transit deep into the Far South Side. But it runs about half a mile west of Roseland's Michigan Avenue spine — so a Michigan Avenue rider heading to Rosemont sees no change: the #34 bus reaches 95th just as fast, and the trip is spent on the Red and Blue Lines the extension doesn't touch. No time saved — and it is about where the tracks run, not whether they help. The Red Line Extension is a major gain for the Far South Side, but its alignment follows the old rail right-of-way a half-mile west of Roseland's Michigan Avenue. For a Michigan Avenue rider the #34 bus reaches 95th just as quickly, and the trip's length is set by the Red and Blue Line rides the extension never touches. The lesson is the same one the CrossTowner maps keep showing from the other direction: new rail helps enormously — for the trips and the people its stations actually reach.
