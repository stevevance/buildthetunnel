Method & sources. Both itineraries were planned by the Conveyal R5 routing engine (via r5r 2.4.0) for Tuesday July 7, 2026, ~8:00 am departures, on the same OpenStreetMap street network. Today's trip uses the published CTA, Metra, and Pace GTFS feeds (R5's best route matches an independent Transitous/MOTIS query to the minute — 77 minutes, two transfers, same 9:03 ME departure, differing only in which downtown bus bridges the terminals). The X2 trip uses a scheduled GTFS feed for CrossTowner routes X1–X6 generated from Star:Line Chicago's timetable ("CrossTowner Schedules" skeleton worksheets), layered onto today's feeds; the feed passes the MobilityData canonical validator with zero errors. Route definition ("Palatine to 93rd/South Chicago, every 30 minutes via tunnel") from buildthetunnelchicago.org. Times shown are rounded to the minute and run from first train departure to last train arrival. One adjustment to R5's raw output: its street-level walk into Millennium Station (about 1 minute) understates the underground concourse, so the card uses ~3 minutes per local knowledge — this consumes the connection's slack but changes neither the 9:03 ME departure nor the total; Station accessibility comes from the feeds' wheelchair_boarding field (all trains are accessible; per the analysis assumption, today's station accessibility carries into the future and new CrossTowner stations are accessible). R5's door-to-door durations (which add a ~2-minute walk-and-wait at Arlington Heights on both trips) are 77 and 62 minutes. Full provenance, parameters, validations, and limitations: Walking is computed at 2.75 mph (4.43 km/h) — between the traffic-engineering design speed and empirical usual pace (Bohannon 1997; Murtagh 2021) — while complex-station walks (concourses, airport security, the O'Hare people mover) are set explicitly rather than gait-limited.
