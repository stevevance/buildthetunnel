Five trips, today and with the CrossTowner

Each page compares a real trip on today's CTA/Metra/Pace network against the same trip on the CrossTowner draft schedule. Both sides are planned by the same routing engine (Conveyal R5) on identical street and schedule data — the only difference is the proposed service. Cards mark out-of-station transfers and station wheelchair accessibility, and airport trips are measured to the Terminal 2 security line.

  1. Berkeley → 123rd StreetThe biggest winner between stations that both exist today: a four-vehicle, ~3-hour trip becomes a one-seat 66-minute X6 ride — 97 minutes saved by the all-day measure.
  2. Arlington Heights → Hyde Park75 minutes and two transfers today; a one-seat 60-minute ride on the X2.
  3. Northwestern → University of ChicagoTwo campuses with no direct rail link today: 69 minutes and two transfers become a one-seat 44-minute ride, four trains an hour.
  4. Evanston → PilsenThe X4 replaces a 12-minute walk across the Loop with a curbside transfer at Union Station.
  5. Beverly → O'HareThe Union Station pulse working as designed: a timed 5-minute transfer, 7-12 minutes saved, and a fully accessible route where today's is not.
  6. South Shore → Botanic GardenLakefront to lakefront: three vehicles and two sparse Metra waits become one X1 train, and Braeside goes from a flag stop to all-day, every-30-minute service.
  7. Downers Grove → O'HareThree vehicles become one seat to the airport's front door — plus the surprising Pace itinerary the router discovered.
  8. Naperville → O'HareAn honest null result: the draft X5 schedule saves no time here, and the fixes are specific and small.
  9. Tinley Park → Lake BluffThe other honest null: no time saved, because Tinley's Rock Island line dead-ends at LaSalle Street — a station the tunnel doesn't connect. A lesson in which lines the plan routes through.
  10. Roseland → RosemontA different project, the same lesson: the under-construction Red Line Extension runs half a mile west of Roseland's Michigan Avenue, so this trip is unchanged. New rail helps the trips its stations actually reach.

The CrossTowner service pattern and timetable are from Star:Line Chicago's proposal. Methodology, data provenance, validations, and limitations are documented in METHODOLOGY.md; the scenario GTFS feed, generator code, and all results are in the analysis folder of this repository.
