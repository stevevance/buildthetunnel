85 minutes faster train-to-arrival — and 106 minutes by the all-day measure. The one-seat X6 replaces four vehicles, two out-of-station transfers, and 38 minutes of walking. Averaged over all departure times (the metric used to rank all 19,738 station pairs), the trip goes from 165 minutes to 59 — the single largest improvement in the network. The mechanism: Berkeley's corridor and the South Side's can only reach each other through two different downtown terminals today; the X6 connects them directly, every 30 minutes, all day.

Method & sources. Both networks routed with Conveyal R5 (via r5r 2.4.0), Tuesday July 7, 2026, ~8:00 am departures, same OpenStreetMap street network; today's feeds are published CTA/Metra/Pace GTFS, the scenario adds the CrossTowner X1–X6 scheduled feed generated from Star:Line Chicago's timetable. Card times are R5's next-departure itineraries; the 165-vs-59-minute figures in the summary are medians across an 8:00–8:30 departure window (max 20-minute walks), the measure used to rank all station pairs — Berkeley's ranking is a floor, since several of its today-times press against the search's 180-minute ceiling. 79th (Auburn Park) is a planned Rock Island infill station; its "today" time is measured from the same street corner over today's network. Station accessibility comes from the feeds' wheelchair_boarding field (all trains are accessible; new CrossTowner stations assumed accessible). Times rounded to the minute.
