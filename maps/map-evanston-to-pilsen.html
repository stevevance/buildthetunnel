Method & sources. Both itineraries were planned by the Conveyal R5 routing engine (via r5r 2.4.0) for Tuesday July 7, 2026, ~8:00 am departures, on the same OpenStreetMap street network. Today's trip uses the published CTA, Metra, and Pace GTFS feeds; the CTA #8 and #18 bus legs come from the CTA feed. The X4 trip adds the scheduled CrossTowner X1–X6 GTFS generated from Star:Line Chicago's timetable ("CrossTowner Schedules" skeleton worksheets); the feed passes the MobilityData canonical validator with zero errors. Route definition ("Winnetka to 115th (Kensington) and Blue Island, every 30 minutes via tunnel") from buildthetunnelchicago.org. Times shown are rounded to the minute and run from first train departure to arrival; R5's door-to-door durations are 56 and 47 minutes. Station accessibility comes from the feeds' wheelchair_boarding field (all trains are accessible; per the analysis assumption, today's station accessibility carries into the future and new CrossTowner stations are accessible). Full provenance, parameters, validations, and limitations: Walking is computed at 2.75 mph (4.43 km/h) — between the traffic-engineering design speed and empirical usual pace (Bohannon 1997; Murtagh 2021) — while complex-station walks (concourses, airport security, the O'Hare people mover) are set explicitly rather than gait-limited.
