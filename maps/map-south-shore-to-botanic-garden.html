South Shore to the Botanic Garden: one train instead of three vehicles

The 71st/Exchange Metra station on the South Side lakefront and the Chicago Botanic Garden, near Braeside on the North Shore, are both a stone's throw from the same regional rail — but connecting them today means three vehicles and two long Metra waits. The CrossTowner X1 runs the South Chicago branch up through the tunnel and onto the North Shore as one train, and it steps Braeside up from roughly hourly midday Metra service to every 30 minutes all day. Routed at midday on both networks by Conveyal R5 on identical data.

12 minutes faster, and three vehicles become one train. The saving is modest because the Botanic Garden sits a mile from the nearest station, so both trips end on foot. But the real gain is directness and frequency: the X1 deletes two of Metra's sparse midday waits and a downtown transfer, replacing a bus-Red-Line-Pace-bus relay with a single lakefront-to-lakefront ride — and it upgrades Braeside from a stop half the trains skip to reliable all-day, every-30-minute service.
