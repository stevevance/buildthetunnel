Tinley Park to Lake Bluff: where the tunnel stops helping

This is a trip the CrossTowner does not fix, and the reason is precise and worth showing. Tinley Park is on the Rock Island's Joliet main line, which dead-ends at LaSalle Street Station — and the CrossTowner tunnel connects Union Station and Ogilvie, not LaSalle Street. So a rider must still make the same downtown terminal walk they make today. The plan routes the Metra Electric and the Rock Island Beverly branch through the tunnel, but not this line. Both networks routed by the same engine (Conveyal R5) on identical data.

No time saved — and it shows what the line choices decide. The CrossTowner turns the northern transfer into a 48-second same-station change at Central Street, exactly the win you would expect. But the southern transfer cannot improve: Tinley Park's Rock Island Joliet line dead-ends at LaSalle Street, and the tunnel connects Union Station and Ogilvie — so the rider makes the same downtown terminal walk as today. The plan routes the Metra Electric and the Rock Island Beverly branch through the tunnel, not this line. Extend it to the Joliet line and Tinley Park would gain what every connected corridor gains; leave it out and the trip stays put.

Method & sources. Both networks routed with Conveyal R5 (via r5r 2.4.0), Tuesday July 7, 2026, ~8:00 am departures, same OpenStreetMap street network; today's feeds are published CTA/Metra/Pace GTFS, the scenario adds the CrossTowner X1–X6 scheduled feed generated from Star:Line Chicago's timetable. R5 returns ~150 minutes on both networks. The scenario's routing is Rock Island → (14-min out-of-station walk, LaSalle Street to Union Station) → X1 → (48-second same-station change at Central St, Evanston) → UP-N; the northern transfer improves, the southern one does not, and the total is a wash. Times rounded to the minute; the ~17-minute Rock Island wait reflects hourly off-peak service. Station accessibility from the feeds' wheelchair_boarding field (all trains accessible). This is one of two deliberately unflattering cases in the set, alongside Naperville → O'Hare — there the barrier is a fixable schedule gap; here it is a line the plan does not route through the tunnel. Walking is computed at 2.75 mph (4.43 km/h) — between the traffic-engineering design speed and empirical usual pace (Bohannon 1997; Murtagh 2021) — while complex-station walks (concourses, airport security, the O'Hare people mover) are set explicitly rather than gait-limited.
