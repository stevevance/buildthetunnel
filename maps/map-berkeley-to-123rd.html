Berkeley to 123rd Street: two Metra stations, three hours apart

Berkeley (UP-W) and 123rd Street in Blue Island (Rock Island) are both on today's Metra map — and the trip between them still takes about three hours, because their lines meet nowhere and their service is thin. Among the 19,738 station pairs we tested, this is the largest improvement where both stations already exist. The CrossTowner X6 makes it one seat. 75 minutes faster train-to-arrival — and 97 minutes by the all-day measure. Both stations exist right now; what's missing is service that connects them. Today the trip needs four vehicles, two out-of-station transfers, 35 minutes of walking, and still ends with a hike from 119th because 123rd's station is served only by six evening-rush trains. Averaged over all departure times — the metric used to rank every pair — the trip goes from 179 minutes to 82, the largest improvement between two existing stations in the network.

Method & sources. Both networks routed with Conveyal R5 (via r5r 2.4.0), Tuesday July 7, 2026, ~8:00 am departures, same OpenStreetMap street network; today's feeds are published CTA/Metra/Pace GTFS, the scenario adds the CrossTowner X1–X6 scheduled feed generated from Star:Line Chicago's timetable. Card times are R5's itineraries for an 8:00 am traveler; the 179-vs-82-minute figures are medians across an 8:00–8:30 departure window (max 20-minute walks), the measure used to rank all 19,738 station pairs — and 179 presses against the search's 180-minute ceiling, so the true saving may be larger. "Existing station" means a Metra or CTA rail station within 0.12 miles of the CrossTowner point (see results/top25_existing_stations_only.csv). Station accessibility comes from the feeds' wheelchair_boarding field (all trains are accessible; Metra codes 123rd St as "no information"). Times rounded to the minute. Walking is computed at 2.75 mph (4.43 km/h) — between the traffic-engineering design speed and empirical usual pace (Bohannon 1997; Murtagh 2021) — while complex-station walks (concourses, airport security, the O'Hare people mover) are set explicitly rather than gait-limited. Full provenance and limitations: METHODOLOGY.md in the BuildTheTunnel repo's analysis folder.
